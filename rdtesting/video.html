    The nuclear revival

    In 1998, nuclear energy research in the US was on the verge of extinction. Funding had all but vanished. With a similarly steep drop in undergraduate enrollment, it looked like US might not play a significant role in the future of nuclear technologies. Michigan engineers helped reverse the trend.

    How crud forms on nuclear fuel

    U-M research for the Consortium for Advanced Simulation of Light Water Reactors (CASL) is already shedding light on a costly problem: how chemical deposits build up on nuclear fuel rods. NERS researchers' findings could help plant operators devise strategies to avoid crud formation.
