The University of Michigan is open to students from all walks of life - whether you’re coming straight from high school, transferring from another college or even from another country.

The college application process takes time and effort - and a lot of information. Even once a decision is made to apply to a school, the process for doing so can be daunting. Let us demystify the experience.

COMMON APPLICATION (NOTE: Jennifer JH will build this)

The Common Application is the standard form required to apply for entry into any undergraduate study. Link

The Paperwork

Applying to U-M requires the following items:

  • The Common Application
  • The U-M Supplement of the Common App
  • High School Transcripts and School Report
  • Teacher Evaluation
  • SAT or ACT with writing test scores
  • Additional items may be needed for international, transfer or home-schooled students.

» See the full list of applications requirements

Application: Important Dates

  • Oct. 1: CSS/Financial Aid Profile Opens
  • Nov. 1: Early Action Deadline
  • Jan. 1: FAFSA Opens
  • Feb. 1: Fall, Spring or Summer Term Deadline
  • Oct. 1: Winter Term Deadline

Enrollment: Important Dates

  • Dec. 24: Early Decisions reached
  • Jan. 1: Fill out FAFSA and CSS/Financial Aid Profile
  • Jan. 1 - April 15: Register for a Campus Day visit
  • Feb. 15: Early Deadline for FAFSA and CSS/Financial Aid Profile
  • April 15: Scholarship status notifications made by this date
  • April 30: Final Deadline for FAFSA and CSS/Financial Aid Profile
  • May 1: Enrollment Deposit Due
  • May 7: Submit Housing/Michigan Learning Community Application

Application Tips

  • Which application?
    • If your student hasn’t yet attended any institute of higher learning post-high school, they should apply for first-year admission.
    • If your student is currently attending a community college, two- or four-year college or university, they should apply for a transfer.
    • If your student intends on transferring from another college or school within the University of Michigan, they should apply for a cross-campus transfer.
    • If your student attended school outside of the U.S., does not speak English as a native language or is a non U.S. Citizen or permanent U.S. Resident, they should apply for an international transfer.
  • Apply early. The best advice we can give you is to get your application in before the deadline. It gives us time to respond to your questions during the process, puts you in a great position for scholarships... and lifts a huge weight off your shoulders.
