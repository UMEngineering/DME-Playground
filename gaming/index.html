Gaming for the Greater Good

  • The inspiration He chalks it up to his Catholic upbringing, but David Chesney can't help but shoehorn some good into unexpected places.
  • A different kind of design class Chesney teaches a senior-level design software engineering course, and the "Gaming for the Greater Good" motive is present there as well, but perhaps with more force since he runs the class like a high tech incubator.
  • Beyond the classroom Teams of students are working with doctors and patients to get the games into the hands of people who can benefit them.
  • The magic of the common good People have come out of the woodwork to help this project. Jackie Kauffman, a psychology researchers, has volunteered to help the students learn about autism.
  • Future possibilities The Kinect device, in future generations, will be able to detect eye movement. Chesney says this might be an opportunity for his class in the future.
