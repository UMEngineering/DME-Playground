DME: Tuberculosis is stubborn, deadly and thriving

About 30 percent of humans probably harbor tuberculosis, the world's most successful pathogen. Now, an immunologist, a U-M microbiologist and a U-M chemical engineer are working on a sim that will help develop new and better treatments.

MconneX: Cars that talk to each other

Cars that communicate might make the roads safer. The U-M Safety Pilot Program in Ann Arbor is conducting the largest ever connected-vehicle, street-level study.



 

Oil Spill Cleanup: Smart filters can strain oil out of water

A filter developed by Michigan Engineers has the unconventional ability to repel oil yet attract water, in effect straining the oil from the water.



 

MconneX: Batteries of the future

Discussing the limitations of current battery technology and the improvements needed to make them viable for large consumer products, Mechanical Engineering Assistant Professor Don Siegel conducts this BrownBag Learning series.



 

Fetchnotes: From students to TechStars

Student-founded startup Fetchnotes, with its cloud-based note-jotting and organizing system, has won a spot in the prestigious business accelerator program TechStars.
